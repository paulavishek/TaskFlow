Understanding Lean Six Sigma Categories
  Value-Added (VA)

Activities that directly contribute to meeting customer requirements and that the customer is willing to pay for.

Examples:
  • Performing actual work/service
  • Transforming a product
  • Creating features customers request

These tasks should be optimized and prioritized.

  Necessary Non-Value-Added (NNVA)

Activities that don't add value from the customer's perspective but are required due to current constraints.

Examples:
  • Regulatory compliance
  • Documentation for legal reasons
  • Quality inspections

These tasks should be minimized but cannot be eliminated entirely.

  Waste/Eliminate (NVA)

Activities that consume resources but add no value from the customer's perspective.

Examples:
  • Waiting time
  • Rework and defects
  • Unnecessary meetings or documentation

These tasks should be eliminated whenever possible.

Why This Matters:

Categorizing tasks this way helps teams identify and eliminate waste, streamline necessary processes, and focus resources on what truly adds value. Aim for a high percentage of Value-Added tasks to improve overall efficiency.
